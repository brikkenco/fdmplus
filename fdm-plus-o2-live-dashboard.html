The important question is not just "is O2 ranking well?" It is which device and channel combinations are creating that visibility, and whether they are durable. If O2's strongest positions are concentrated in a few devices, any price, promotion or availability movement on those devices could materially affect the overall ranking story.

Recommended action

Create device-level watchlists for the strongest O2 visibility drivers, starting with Galaxy S26 256GB, Galaxy S26 Ultra 256GB, iPhone 17 256GB and iPhone 17 Pro Max.

For each watchlist, track rank position, retailer, price, total cost, promotion type, availability and nearest competitor. Prioritise devices where O2 is #1 but not the cheapest, because those positions may depend on promotion, retailer placement or value perception rather than price leadership.

Which promotions have an end date or recent change?

The northern concern is that high-ranked visibility is not translating evenly into regional share. O2's affiliate visibility is also weak in the latest newsletter cuts, with 23% SOV in the broader newsletter cut and 30% SOV in the narrower cut, with only three O2 plans featured.

Interpretation

The northern market-share issue looks like a combination of price pressure, channel visibility and promotion dependency.

Three is the most important immediate threat on flagship Apple. Current pricing evidence shows Three as the most competitive provider on iPhone 17 Pro Max despite price decreases from O2 and EE. That matters because flagship Apple is a visible comparison point, and northern share pressure appears strongest where customers are more price-sensitive.

O2 also has a channel issue. O2 is strong in selected specialist and direct channels, but weaker in comparison and affiliate environments. Fonehouse and Carphone Warehouse show no O2 rows in the current captured ranking view, while Money Saving Expert and MoneySuperMarket are weak or SIMO/value-led environments for O2.

The promotion story is more nuanced. O2 has high promotion-supported visibility at 92.5%, but that does not mean the promotions are strong enough to close regional value gaps.
